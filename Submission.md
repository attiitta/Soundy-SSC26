### What problem is your app playground trying to solve and what inspired you to solve it? 200 words or less.
このSoundyというアプリは耳が不自由な人たちが実際に抱えている孤独感というものを音楽という視点から世界と関わりを持つことができればいいなという思いで作りました。

なぜこのような問題の原点は私の弟が関わっています。彼は音楽的センスがあり特にバイオリンのセンスに関しては他とは違う異色の輝きを放ってました。また僕たち兄弟はよく一緒にセッションしてました。しかし少し前に親の会話から彼が課題曲をイヤホンで聴きすぎたことやバイオリンの練習のしすぎで耳が徐々に悪くなってきているとわかりました。僕は音楽への情熱や好意を失わなければいけなくなるのは不条理だなと思いました。

何がこの問題への解決の方法を導かせたのかというと、それは友達と何気なく話してたライブについてのやりとりでした。その友達はとある日本のアーティストのファンでよくライブに行っていました。その時これは何か解決方法につながるかもしれないと思い僕はライブって何で楽しいのと聞いてみました。出てきた答えがライトと振動です。なので耳の不自由抜きに楽しめるライトとバイブレーションを使ったアプリを作りました。
I created the Soundy app with the hope that it would help address the sense of loneliness that deaf people often feel, by allowing them to connect with the world through music.

The origin of this problem lies with my younger brother. He had a great musical talent, and his talent for the violin in particular was unique and exceptional. My brother and I often jammed together. However, a while ago, I heard from our parents that his hearing was gradually deteriorating due to listening to assigned pieces with headphones and practicing the violin too much. I thought it was absurd that someone would have to lose their passion and love for music.

What led me to find a solution to this problem was a casual conversation I was having with a friend about live shows. My friend was a fan of a certain Japanese artist and often went to their shows. Thinking that this might lead to a solution, I asked him what it was about live shows that made them so fun. The answer he gave me was lights and vibrations. So I created an app that uses lights and vibrations to be enjoyed regardless of hearing impairment.





### Who would benefit from your app playground and how? 200 words or less.
このアプリは弟をはじめとした世界に五億人近くいる耳が不自由な人や不自由になりかけている人、また不自由ではない人でも一緒に音楽を楽しめるアプリで、そのために行った実装としては、
This app allows nearly 500 million people worldwide—including my brother—who are deaf or hard of hearing, as well as those without hearing impairments, to enjoy music together. To achieve this, we implemented features like vibration feedback so users can feel the rhythm and groove even without hearing the music. We also enhanced colors and added wave patterns to make anyone feel immersed in the music, even without actually hearing it. However, simply feeling the music might feel insufficient or lacking in stimulation for those who originally played instruments. To allow users to actively create music while interacting with the app, we added vibration and color to the flowing particles. Furthermore, to help users visualize each musical part more easily, we set background colors corresponding to the imagery of each part.






### How did accessibility factor into your design process? 200 words or less.
このアプリはそもそもとして耳が不自由な人のために作られたアプリなのであってデザイン一つ一つにこだわりがある。まず、アプリのオンボーディングを音ではなく全て字を書くことにした。これにより耳の不自由な人でもルールがわかる。また全体的に強めな触覚や視覚への刺激を使うことにより聴覚による刺激の少なさから生じる、物足りなさを極限まで減らすように努めた。またバイブレーションのみではなく、後ろの色にも少し激しめの動きをつけたことによりより耳が聞こえない人でも音楽を感じやすい孤独感を減らせるもとになる設計をした。
This app was originally created for the hearing impaired, so every detail of the design was carefully considered. First, we decided to have the app's onboarding be entirely written rather than audio. This allows even the hearing impaired to understand the rules. We also tried to minimize the sense of dissatisfaction that can arise from a lack of auditory stimulation by using strong tactile and visual stimuli overall. We also designed the app to use not only vibrations but also slightly more dynamic background colors, making it easier for hearing impaired people to experience the music and reducing the sense of loneliness.
